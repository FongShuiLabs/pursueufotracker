Correction posted 2026-05-20. An earlier version of this page (published 2026-05-11, last edited 2026-05-14) characterized war.gov's revision as including "5 true removals", "3 true additions", and a "6 Arabian Gulf entries removed / 7 Middle East entries added" geographic consolidation.

A re-audit of the current war.gov CSV (158 rows, SHA-256 0d7e9ba1d5) against our May 8 manifest snapshot found those characterizations to be wrong. None of the 5 entries we described as "removed" are actually removed - all 5 still appear in the current CSV with their original geographic specificity preserved in the Incident Location column, but with broader theater labels (Middle East, Africa, INDOPACOM) in the Title column. The "3 new additions" we listed are the same entries under their new theater-level titles, not net-new files.

What actually happened: war.gov did a title-reformat in which country-level specifics moved from Title into the Incident Location field, and titles adopted broader theater labels. The net row-count delta is real (-3), but it comes from sources we have not yet isolated, not from outright removals. This page has been rewritten below to reflect the corrected reading. The 161 original detail pages on this site remain accessible at their original URLs (URL stability is a deliberate editorial position; see /faq).

1. New DOW-UAP-PR## prefix on 28 entries

War.gov introduced a DOW-UAP-PR## ("PURSUE Report") prefix on 28 entries in the May 11 revision. These join the 41 pre-existing DOW-UAP-D## ("DOW mission report") entries that were in PURSUE from the May 8 release. The corpus now contains 69 zero-padded titles total. The underlying files (PDF/MP4 bytes), source URLs, and SHA-256 hashes for entries we have re-checked are unchanged.

2. Title reformat: theater label in Title, country in Incident Location

The most journalistically interesting pattern in this revision is structural, not deletional. Eleven entries that originally carried a specific country or sea name in the Title field now carry a broader theater-level label, while the original country/sea name appears in the Incident Location column. We previously, mistakenly, described some of these as removals and some as additions. They are the same entries under a relabel.

4. Net row-count delta: -3 (161 -> 158)

The CSV row count dropped by 3 between May 8 and May 11. We have not isolated where the 3-row decrement comes from. Candidates: the six "Arabian Gulf" titles collapsed to six (rather than seven) new "Middle East 2020" titles in our earlier table is one possibility, but our title-to-title mapping is incomplete; some entries may have been de-duplicated against existing rows, and at least one row's relationship to its May 8 ancestor is ambiguous. We are continuing the row-by-row diff and will update this page when complete.
