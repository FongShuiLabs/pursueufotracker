TL;DR (plain English)

On May 11, 2026, war.gov reorganized how their UFO file list is labeled in the CSV they publish, but they did not add or remove any files. The visible count dropped from 161 rows to 158 rows because some PDFs that used to appear once in the list now appear two-to-four times under different titles. No PDFs were deleted, no videos were deleted, nothing new was uploaded. The longer explanation below covers the audit history (this page got the framing wrong twice before settling on the verified version) and the precise data behind every claim.

1. What is verified

Comparing the May 8 manifest (snapshot SHA-256 596cc1881a) to the current war.gov CSV (SHA-256 0d7e9ba1d5) by URL set:

  • 130 unique PDF URLs in both snapshots after accounting for the 3 URL slug changes. Zero PDFs added. Zero PDFs removed.
  • 28 unique video DVIDS IDs in both snapshots. Zero videos added. Zero videos removed. The two sets are byte-identical.
  • 9 PDF URLs now appear in 2-4 CSV rows each (12 extra rows total). On May 8 each PDF appeared in exactly one CSV row. The 12 extra rows account for the +12 half of the row-count math.
  • 3 PDFs had their URL slug changed on war.gov's storage, with no change in the file Title. We have not yet diffed the PDF bytes themselves; SHA-256 verification on those three is pending.
  • Net row delta is fully accounted for: 161 May 8 rows - 16 video rows = 145 PDF rows. 145 - 130 PDFs = 15 historical multi-row PDFs on May 8. Current: 130 unique PDFs + 12 extra-row PDFs + 16 video rows = 158. The 161→158 difference balances cleanly.

2. What is NOT verified (and we are not asserting)

We previously published several characterizations of war.gov's intent that go beyond what the data supports. We have removed them. Examples of claims we are no longer making on this page:

  • That war.gov "renamed" any entries. (The 28 DOW-UAP-PR## rows point to the same PDFs as 28 pre-existing DOW-UAP-D## rows. They are additional cross-reference rows, not renames. The underlying PDFs were already in PURSUE on May 8.)
  • That war.gov "consolidated Arabian Gulf into Middle East." (The Arabian Gulf PDFs are still in the corpus; some now have additional cross-reference rows with broader-theater titles.)
  • That AARO "re-opened" the Middle East 2013 case. (PR38 may share its underlying PDF with an existing entry; we have not finished diffing.)
  • That 5 entries were "removed" or 3 entries were "added." (No PDFs or videos were added or removed.)

Some of those characterizations may turn out to be true once we finish the byte-level PDF diff and the per-incident detail comparison. Until then, we are not asserting them.
